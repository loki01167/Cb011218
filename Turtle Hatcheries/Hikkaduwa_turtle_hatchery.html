HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

GALLERY

The hatchery was founded in 1986 and is situated in Hikkaduwa, a coastal community renowned for its stunning beaches and coral reefs. By gathering and incubating turtle eggs and releasing hatchlings back into the ocean, the hatchery's staff and volunteers aim to save sea turtles from extinction. The hatchery also functions as a facility for the treatment and rehabilitation of ill or damaged turtles before their release back into the wild.

The Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle are among the sea turtle species that can be found in Sri Lanka. Visitors to the hatchery can take a guided tour and learn more about these and other sea turtle species. The tour offers a chance to hear about the hatchery's conservation initiatives and get a close-up look at some of the resident turtles.

In addition to its conservation activities, the hatchery also studies sea turtles and their ecosystems and seeks to spread awareness of how crucial it is to preserve these recognizable and vulnerable animals. Visitors and other groups are encouraged to give to the hatchery in order to support its vital conservation efforts.

THINGS TO DO IN HIKKADUWA

SURFING


With a clear, warm water base and waves as high as 11 feet, surfing is one of the most well-liked activities in Hikkaduwa. The main reef at Hikkaduwa is frequently packed when the circumstances are good because it offers a high-performance wave, an A-frame peak with a significant line extending through to the beach, and a left break into a channel with the best combination of sand and reef. Benny's, which favors the left and is more quicker and hollower than the main reef, is located about 200 m south of the main reef. The dry season, which runs from November through March, is when the four main surf spots in Hikkaduwa offer the finest conditions for surfing. One of the top surfing places in Sri Lanka, Madiha, is located on the southern edge of Hikkaduwa because to the flexible and varied variety of tides in this region, which also contains some beach breaks.

SNORKELLING & SCUBA DIVING


Hikkaduwa's stunning coral reef and rich underwater ecology make it a well-liked destination for snorkeling and scuba diving trips in Sri Lanka. The fringing bank of the Hikkaduwa National Park is home to approximately 60 types of hard coral and around 170 different varieties of tropical reef fish. Visitors get the chance to examine a variety of shipwrecks during these trips, some of which are located at various ocean depths and are well-maintained. The finest months to go snorkeling and scuba diving at Hikkaduwa are November through April, when the sea is calm and the lighting is ideal for mesmerizing underwater shots.

BEACH FUN


Although the beaches of Hikkaduwa may not compare to those in Goa or Bali, they nonetheless draw a good number of tourists from across the world! With a beachfront that spans about 4 kilometers, the Narigama beach is known for drawing large crowds and is the busiest surfing area from April through October. Here, the setting sun spills gorgeous colours, creating for a delightful spectacle for everyone.
